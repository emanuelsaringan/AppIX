Law of Universal Gravitation

You throw a ball up, it will come down and the force which pulls objects down is the force of gravity.

A law formulated by Isaac Newton in 1687 which states that two object with masses m1 and m2 are separated by a distance r, a gravitational force F acts along a line joining them, with magnitude given by

F = G m 1 m 2 r 2

Where G = 6.67 x 10-11 is the gravitational constant measured by Henry Cavendish using the torsion balance.

  • Gravity is a force that attracts.
  • Anything that has mass has gravity. It's just that some objects doesn't have enough mass to produce a very strong gravitational force that can be felt by our senses.

Weight vs. Mass

The term weight that we use in our daily basis is not the same as the physics definition of weight. Most people define weight as the same as mass, but physics defines weight as the product of the mass and the acceleration due to gravity of Earth.

Weight = mass x gravity W = m g

However mass is,

M = p(density) x volume

Weight is measured in Newtons and requires gravity while mass is in Kilograms and only needs the object itself.
