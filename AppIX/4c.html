Speed vs Velocity

Speed answers the question how fast an object moves or the distance the he travels over time duration. It is also a scalar quantity which uses basic arithmetic to arrive at the final answer.

Speed (v) = Distance (m) Time(s) Speed (v) = m / s

It's simply distance over time.
To broaden up, let's use again the dog problem in distance and displacement. We know that the distance between the two points is 50 m, if the dog can run the distance in 10 seconds, then what would be his speed?

V = 50 m 10 s v = 5 m / s

The dog can run the whole track in a speed of 5 m/s.

Velocity, however is a vector quantity, it considers both magnitude and the direction of the object.
Velocity can be defined operationally as the “displacement” travelled by an object over a time duration.

v = d(displacement) t(time)

So if the car in the recent problem could travel the whole course in 5 seconds then,

V = 50 m 5 s

Then its velocity would be 10 m/s
