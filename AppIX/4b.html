Distance vs Displacement

Distance is the magnitude of how far the object changed its position or how far the object travelled. For example, the distance from point A to point B is 50 m. if you let a dog travel point A to point B then the dog will travel a distance of 50 m, as defined by the given distance.


Let's change the scenario, if the dog travels from point A to point B, then moves to point C. After some time, the dog senses a bone at point D then runs after it. If A to B is 50 m and B to C is 50 m and C to D is 75 m, what is the total distance travelled by the ball?

DT = 50m + 50m + 75m = 175 m is the total distance travelled by the dog.

Since distance is a scalar quantity, simple arithmetic is used.

On the other hand, displacement is somehow similar to distance "but", it now considers direction, meaning it is a vector quantity. Displacement can be defined as the change between the final and initial position of an object.

The only thing that matters in displacement is the initial and final position. The red arrow indicates the displacement of the object.

If a car travels from point A to point B and point B to point C, AB is 30 m and BC is 40 m. Then what would be its displacement?
Obviously, we can see it as a right triangle so we solve it using Pythagorean Theorem.

A B = 30 m B C = 40 m A C = 30 2 + 40 2 A C = 50 m

The displacement* of the object is 50 m.

*Displacement can also be solved using trigonometry.
