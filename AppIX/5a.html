Free Fall Acceleration

Free fall refers to the motion of a falling body under the influence of gravity alone, no other external forces. Though air drag can be a factor, it can be considered as negligible depending upon the air column created. Try dropping a piece of paper (try choosing an are with less wind) at a height the same as the length of your nail, if you are to observe the paper wasn't affected by air at all because of the small amount of air column, therefore we can neglect air and consider it as free fall. But dropping it at a building isn't free fall anymore because the air affects the paper at a large rate causing it to sway.

Solving free fall problems is the same as solving problems of Uniformly Accelerated Motion; the only difference is that you use acceleration due to gravity (g) instead of acceleration (a) and equations 1, 3, and 4 are used.

Where g = 9.80 m/s2

Equation 1: V = V 0 + g t

Equation 3: V 2 = V 0 2 + 2 g d

Equation 4: d = V 0 t + 1 2 g t 2

Meaning, an object freely falls with an acceleration of 9.80 m/s2 only in Earth. Acceleration due to gravity may change depending upon the gravitational field of a certain object, let's say the moon, would have lesser gravitational force that Earth because of lesser mass.

There was once a tale about Galileo dropping two objects with different masses at the leaning tower of pisa, trying to prove that both would fall at the same rate because the acceleration of both objects are similar.

There are three factors to know if an object is in free fall here on Earth:

  1. No other external forces are present rather than gravity
  2. Air resistance should be negligible in order to achieve free fall.
  3. It is within the height lesser than the radius of the Earth.

Sample Problem

A ball is dropped from a building 10 m high. How much time would it take to reach the ground?

Given: D = 10 m g = 9.8 m/s2 V = 0 m/s

Using the 4th equation in UAM(uniformly accelerated motion):

d = V 0 t + 1 2 g t 2

where V0 = 0 m/s

d = 1 2 g t 2

Deriving time (t)

t = 2d g = 2(10.0 m) 9.80 m/s2 t = 1.43

The time that it would take is 1.43 s

Another problem:

A ball is thrown vertically with a speed of 10.0 m/s at a height of 20.0 m. What would be its velocity when it hits the ground?

Given: D = 20.0 m g = 9.80 m/s2 V = 10 m/s

Using the 3rd equation in UAM(uniformly accelerated motion):

V 2 = V 0 2 + 2 g d V 2 = 10.02 + 2 9.80 m/s2 20.0 m V 2 = 296 V 2 = 17.2 m/s

The final velocity is 17.2 m/s
