Acceleration

Acceleration is defined as how fast velocity changes over a period of time.

It is simply speeding up or slowing down.


An applied force can accelerate an object, depending on the magnitude of the force, this scenario will be further discussed in Newton's 2nd law.

acceleration is defined operationally as:

a = V - V 0 t

Acceleration can be further explained using a velocity-time graph. The graph shows the relatioship between the change in velocity in a unit time.

Let's try an example

A car has an initial velocity of 10 m/s and accelerates to 50 m/s in a span of 2.00 s. What is the acceleration of the car?

Given:

V = 50 m/s , V 0 = 10 m/s , t = 2.00 s

a = V 0 - V t a = 50 - 10 m/s 2.00 s a = 20 m/s2

The car speeds up 20 m per second in a span of 1 second.
