Second Law of Motion

Law of Acceleration

The acceleration of a body is directly proportional to, and in the same direction as the net force acting on the body and inversely proportional to its mass. Thus, F = ma, where F is the net force acting on the object, m is the mass of the object and a is the acceleration of the object.

This could be expressed mathematically as:

F = m dv dt = m a
Or simply...

F = m a

where mass (m) with its SI unit kg and acceleration (a) described as m/s2 forming

F = kg m s 2

where kg-m/s2 = Newton (N)

the SI unit for force.

Let's try a problem:

A boy pushes a box with a mass of 5 kg and accelerates at a rate of 0.1 m/s2. How much force did the boy apple to the box?

Box (m) = 5 kg A = 0.1 m/s2 F = m a F = (5kg) (0.1m/s2) F = 0.5 N
